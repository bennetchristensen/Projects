Get to know me

My name is Bennet Christensen and I am a statistics and geography double major with a concentration
in community and global health at Macalester College in Saint Paul, currently in my junior year.

I am passionate about data visualization, especially mapping data. I find statistical applications to
health and economics especially meaningful to work on due to their imediate impact and implications
on individaul's quality of life. My most recent projects are a spatial risk analysis of narcotic arrests in
Seattle, my capstone project from correlated data, a survival analysis on brain cancer, my capstone
project from survival analysis, and an independent project on defence contractors that is still a work
in progress. Most of the work on that project so far has been teching myself webscraping, but a brief
one page preliminary findings is available to look at. Unless hidden for privacy reasons, all
my projects including this website are viewable on my github.

Before Macalester I attended Walter Johnson High School in Bethesda, Maryland, where I was in the
top 5% of my class. I was a member of our debate team as well as the model UN team. While my
interests eventually diverted away from the humanities, my experiences with these teams were
invaluable in developing my public speaking abilities.
